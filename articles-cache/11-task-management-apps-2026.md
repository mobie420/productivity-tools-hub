## The Task Management Landscape

A good task manager should disappear into your workflow, not add overhead. Here's what's working in 2026.

## 1. **Todoist** – The All‑Rounder

**Best for:** Most people who need reliability and simplicity.

**Key features:**  
- Natural language input ("Buy milk tomorrow at 5pm")  
- Projects, labels, filters  
- Karma points for gamification  

**Pricing:** Free for basic; Pro $4/month.

**Why we recommend it:** The most polished balance of power and simplicity.

[**Try Todoist**](https://todoist.com) *(affiliate link)*

## 2. **Things 3** – Apple Ecosystem Perfection

**Best for:** Apple‑only users who value design.

**Key features:**  
- Beautiful native apps (iOS, Mac, iPad)  
- Areas of responsibility  
- Today/Upcoming/Someday views  

**Pricing:** $49.99 Mac + $19.99 iOS (one‑time).

**Why we recommend it:** The most delightful task management experience on Apple devices.

[**Get Things 3**](https://culturedcode.com/things) *(affiliate link)*

## 3. **TickTick** – Feature‑Packed Value

**Best for:** Power users who want calendar integration and habit tracking.

**Key features:**  
- Built‑in calendar view  
- Habit tracker  
- Eisenhower matrix  
- Pomodoro timer  

**Pricing:** Free for basic; Premium $2.79/month.

**Why we recommend it:** Incredible value with features others charge extra for.

[**Try TickTick**](https://ticktick.com) *(affiliate link)*

## 4. **Microsoft To Do** – Simple & Free

**Best for:** Microsoft 365 users and minimalists.

**Key features:**  
- Completely free  
- My Day feature  
- Integrates with Outlook tasks  

**Pricing:** Free.

**Why we recommend it:** Perfect if you're already in the Microsoft ecosystem.

[**Get Microsoft To Do**](https://todo.microsoft.com) *(non‑affiliate)*

## 5. **Amazing Marvin** – Customizable Psychology

**Best for:** People who need to tweak everything.

**Key features:**  
- 50+ strategies to enable/disable  
- Time tracking  
- Focus mode  
- ADHD‑friendly features  

**Pricing:** $12/month or $96/year.

**Why we recommend it:** Adapts to your psychology instead of forcing you into a system.

[**Try Amazing Marvin**](https://amazingmarvin.com) *(affiliate link)*

## 6. **Remember The Milk** – The Veteran

**Best for:** Long‑time users and keyboard shortcut lovers.

**Key features:**  
- Extensive keyboard shortcuts  
- Smart Lists (powerful searching)  
- Location‑based reminders  

**Pricing:** Free for basic; Pro $39.99/year.

**Why we recommend it:** Still excellent for keyboard‑first power users.

[**Try Remember The Milk**](https://rememberthemilk.com) *(affiliate link)*

## 7. **Any.do** – Voice‑First Simplicity

**Best for:** People who prefer voice input.

**Key features:**  
- Excellent voice recognition  
- WhatsApp integration for tasks  
- Clean, simple interface  

**Pricing:** Free for basic; Premium $2.99/month.

**Why we recommend it:** The best voice input experience.

[**Try Any.do**](https://any.do) *(affiliate link)*
