## The Note‑Taking Methodology Landscape

Collecting notes is easy. Building a system that helps you think and create is hard.

## 1. **PARA Method** – Project‑Based Organization

**What it is:** Organizes notes into Projects, Areas, Resources, and Archives.

**Best for:** People with active projects and clear responsibilities.

**Tools:** Notion, Obsidian, Evernote.

**Key insight:** Information should be organized by what it's for, not what it's about.

## 2. **Zettelkasten** – Atomic Thinking

**What it is:** Each note contains one idea, linked to other ideas to form knowledge networks.

**Best for:** Researchers, writers, and deep thinkers.

**Tools:** Obsidian, Roam Research, Logseq.

**Key insight:** Knowledge grows through connections, not just accumulation.

## 3. **Bullet Journal** – Analog‑Digital Hybrid

**What it is:** Rapid logging system with migration and reflection.

**Best for:** People who want mindfulness and intentionality.

**Tools:** Physical notebook + digital backup (Evernote, Notion).

**Key insight:** Regular review is more important than perfect organization.

## 4. **CODE Framework** – Capture, Organize, Distill, Express

**What it is:** Four‑stage workflow from collecting to creating.

**Best for:** Content creators and knowledge workers.

**Tools:** Readwise, Notion, Obsidian.

**Key insight:** Notes should progress from raw capture to polished output.

## Implementation Guide

**Step 1 – Choose your methodology:**
- **If you have projects:** PARA
- **If you do research:** Zettelkasten  
- **If you want mindfulness:** Bullet Journal
- **If you create content:** CODE

**Step 2 – Pick one tool:** Start with Notion (easiest) or Obsidian (most powerful).

**Step 3 – Weekly review:** Spend 30 minutes every Friday organizing and connecting notes.

**Step 4 – Create something:** Use your notes to write an article, make a decision, or solve a problem.

## Common Pitfalls

- **Tool hopping:** Pick one and stick with it for 3 months.
- **Over‑organizing:** Focus on retrieval, not taxonomy.
- **Not reviewing:** Unreviewed notes are wasted notes.
