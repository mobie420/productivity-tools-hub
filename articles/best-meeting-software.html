The Remote‑Meeting Landscape in 2026

Video calls are no longer just about seeing faces – they’re about collaboration, engagement, and integration.

The Top Contenders

1. Zoom – The Reliable Workhorse

Why it’s still king: Rock‑stable, ubiquitous, and packed with features (breakout rooms, polling, transcription).

Pricing: Free for 40‑minute group meetings; paid plans from $15/month/host.

Best for: Companies that need reliability and wide adoption.

Get Zoom (affiliate link)

2. Gather – The Virtual Office

Why it’s unique: Spatial audio, virtual office floors, and casual “water‑cooler” moments.

Pricing: Free for up to 25 concurrent users; paid from $7/user/month.

Best for: Teams that miss office serendipity.

Try Gather (affiliate link)

3. Whereby – The No‑Download Simplicity

Why it’s great: Runs entirely in the browser. No app installs for participants.

Pricing: Free for 1 room with 4 participants; paid from $10/month.

Best for: Client calls and quick ad‑hoc meetings.

Try Whereby (affiliate link)

4. Microsoft Teams – The Enterprise Suite

Why it’s powerful: Deep integration with Office 365, SharePoint, and Power BI.

Pricing: Included with Microsoft 365 (from $6/user/month).

Best for: Organizations already in the Microsoft ecosystem.

Get Microsoft Teams (affiliate link)

5. Slack Huddles – The Lightweight Alternative

Why it’s handy: Instant voice/video within Slack channels. No scheduling needed.

Pricing: Included with Slack (free tier has 2‑person huddles).

Best for: Teams that live in Slack and need quick syncs.

Try Slack (affiliate link)

How to Choose

  • For large all‑hands: Zoom
  • For team culture: Gather
  • For client calls: Whereby
  • For enterprise integration: Teams
  • For instant Slack‑first chats: Slack Huddles
